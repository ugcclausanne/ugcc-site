About us

History of Ukrainian Pastoral Care in Switzerland

Ukrainian church life in Switzerland is closely linked to the history of emigration from Ukraine, which intensified after the First World War. Among those who found refuge here was Yevhen Konovalets, a prominent figure of the liberation movement.

After World War II, the Ukrainian community in Switzerland grew, creating a need for permanent pastoral care. On February 4, 1951, Deacon Danylo Dzvonyk was ordained priest in the cathedral of Fribourg by Bishop Ivan Buchko, Exarch for Ukrainians abroad. For several years Fr. Danylo ministered across Switzerland and was active in the Ukrainian Society of Switzerland.

In the 1960s, Fr. Myroslav Marusin continued the pastoral mission. Later the Apostolic Exarchate in France was established, caring also for Ukrainians in Switzerland. In 2016 Fr. Volodymyr Horoshko arrived in Switzerland, followed by other priests who fostered parish life in various cities.

Today, Ukrainian pastoral care in Switzerland unites communities across the cantons, forming a living church environment that helps preserve faith and cultural heritage.
